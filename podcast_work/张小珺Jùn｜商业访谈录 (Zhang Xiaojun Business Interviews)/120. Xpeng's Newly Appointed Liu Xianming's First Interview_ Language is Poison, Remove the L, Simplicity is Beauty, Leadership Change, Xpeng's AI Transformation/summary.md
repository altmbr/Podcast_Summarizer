# Podcast Summary: Liu Xianming on Xiaopeng's AI-Driven Autonomous Driving Transformation

## 1. Key Themes

### Theme 1: Radical Simplification Through Systematic Removal of Legacy Components
Liu Xianming's core strategy involves aggressively removing traditional autonomous driving components to enable pure AI scaling. He systematically dismantled LiDAR, rule-based systems, domain controllers, and most controversially, language tokens from the model architecture.

**Substantiation:**
"从去年到今年，拆了激光雷达，拆了规矩规则，端端端也拆了，然后再后面拆域演，基本全拆了。为什么AI的竞争都是要不断的拆掉更多类优的东西" [00:00:34] (Translation: "From last year to this year, we removed LiDAR, removed rules, removed end-to-end, then removed domain controllers - basically removed everything. Why does AI competition require continuously removing more traditional things")

The most significant removal was language tokens from their VLA model: "我们没有这么用...核心原因还是...你中间始终有Language这个地方去做为你的Supervision...你一定要去生成这个Supervision" [00:21:36] (Translation: "We don't use it that way... the core reason is that you always have Language in the middle as your supervision signal... you must generate this supervision")

### Theme 2: Data as the Fundamental Competitive Moat for OEMs
Liu emphasizes that automakers' unique advantage lies in massive, controllable data collection capabilities, not just technology. This is why he chose Xiaopeng over Robotaxi companies.

**Substantiation:**
"我来小鹏最大的原因...我的核心的逻辑是什么？我来小鹏的核心逻辑是数据...主机厂最大的优势就在于数据" [01:13:06] (Translation: "My biggest reason for joining Xiaopeng... what's my core logic? My core logic for joining Xiaopeng is data... the OEM's biggest advantage lies in data")

He contrasts this with third-party suppliers: "对于第三方来说，它是很难规模化的拿到你想要的数据的...我今天有一个模型上出现了一个问题，我可以自动化一个去找到...明天就会收上来数据" [01:13:28] (Translation: "For third parties, it's very difficult to obtain the data you want at scale... if my model has a problem today, I can automatically identify it... tomorrow the data will be collected")

### Theme 3: Organizational Transformation from Manufacturing to AI Company
Xiaopeng's transformation requires fundamental changes in organizational structure, timeline expectations, and risk tolerance - moving from manufacturing's predictability to AI's uncertainty.

**Substantiation:**
"一个製造業，你關注的事情，你會覺得是遲到、成本...你跟一個製造業的企業去直接據說，我要繼續內容的模型，這個模型可能一年之後才能看到結果，然後這個結果還是我不可預期了，投入多少錢，這個錢又不是我去實體買的" [01:22:54] (Translation: "In manufacturing, you focus on delivery times and costs... if you tell a manufacturing company directly that you want to train a model, this model might only show results after a year, and the results are unpredictable, how much money to invest, and this money doesn't buy physical assets")

Liu implemented extreme organizational flattening: "我們變平化了...所以就是有很多人說非常痕臟，原因就是我一直變平到最一線單位內，我可以看他們的代碼，看他們的實驗結果" [01:27:19] (Translation: "We flattened the organization... many people say it's very hands-on, the reason is I flatten all the way to the frontline, I can look at their code and experimental results")

## 2. Contrarian Perspectives

### Perspective 1: Language Tokens Are Counterproductive for Physical World AI
Liu argues that while language models help AI, using language tokens as an intermediate representation creates a bottleneck that fundamentally limits physical world AI performance - contrary to most VLA approaches.

**Substantiation:**
"既然COT能work，那我们的一个想法是，使用了一层COT...我让这个COT也只做了一个影空间，去看看它对这个世界的理解怎么样" [00:41:42] (Translation: "Since COT works, our idea is to use a layer of COT... but I let this COT only work in visual space to see how it understands the world")

"物理世界的模型，它有两种最大的不一样的东西，一个就是输入，它输入是连续的视觉信号...它的输出也是个连续的空置空间...连续控制量用语言区生成离散的空间化这样的一个Token，再去翻译出来这样连续控制量，显然就是一个低效的一个过程" [00:22:10] (Translation: "Physical world models have two major differences: input is continuous visual signals... output is also continuous control space... using language to generate discrete tokens then translating back to continuous control is obviously an inefficient process")

### Perspective 2: Rules Must Be Completely Eliminated, Not Gradually Reduced
Unlike competitors who keep safety rules as guardrails, Liu advocates for complete rule removal to enable true AI learning - a position that caused significant internal resistance.

**Substantiation:**
"现在我马最后一场规则也拆了...基本没有...已经没有了" [00:58:02] (Translation: "Now I've removed the very last rules... basically none... all gone")

When asked about efficiency in parking scenarios with rules: "也都拆掉...对 拆掉拆掉...然后今年拆了语言" [00:58:14] (Translation: "Also removed... yes, removed removed... then this year removed language")

The resistance was substantial: "其实比较难...就是你总是需要一段時間去查世紀了解這個公司的業務現在的狀況" [01:24:20] (Translation: "It's quite difficult... you always need time to actually understand the company's current business situation")

### Perspective 3: AI Companies Should Organize Around Small Elite Teams, Not Large Distributed Teams
Contrary to ByteDance's model of distributed talent and six-month cycles, Liu argues AI requires concentrated resources in small teams with long-term commitments.

**Substantiation:**
"做AI人的公司，會跟傳統會員好像企業很大的不一樣的地方，就是需要一個核心的團隊，這個團隊要保持穩定，平時充足的資源分配" [01:19:55] (Translation: "AI companies are very different from traditional internet companies - they need a core team that remains stable with sufficient resource allocation")

Comparing to Meituan/ByteDance: "MITAN還有一個問題，就是MITAN...人才密都非常的高...這個會幫助他在之前的互聯網是來快速的去做各種事實和各種創新...但是說AI一定是需要一個體中化的小型化團隊用足夠的資源來去支持" [01:24:45] (Translation: "Meituan has another issue - very high talent density... this helped them quickly do various things in the internet era... but AI definitely needs centralized small teams with sufficient resources")

## 5. Operating Insights

### Insight 1: Deliberately Tolerate Short-Term Performance Regression for Long-Term Capability
Liu actively managed stakeholder expectations for temporary performance drops when removing legacy systems, trading short-term metrics for architectural superiority.

**Quote:** "有可能讓你轉氣會不會退，因為你也反正去年，差不多去年的七八月份吧...你拆掉這個東西的話，你一定是有回退了...但是您可以通過不停的數據節帶，走OTA的節帶，讓它主建拉回來變得更好，就是上線可能會更高" [00:45:31] (Translation: "There might be regression... if you remove this thing, there will definitely be regression... but you can continuously iterate through data and OTA to pull it back up and make it better - the ceiling will be higher")

### Insight 2: Extreme Organizational Flattening Enables Rapid AI Iteration
Liu removed management layers to personally review code and experiments at the frontline, enabling daily rapid decision cycles impossible in traditional hierarchies.

**Quote:** "我們變平化了...所以就是有很多人說非常痕臟，原因就是我一直變平到最一線單位內，我可以看他們的代碼，看他們的實驗結果...大家就在辦公室裡面，隨時去討論問題，有了新的想法，周快速的做出捲測" [01:27:19] (Translation: "We flattened... many say it's very hands-on, because I flatten to the frontline, I can see their code and results... everyone in the office discusses problems anytime, has new ideas, quickly validates")

### Insight 3: Infrastructure Investment Must Precede Model Scaling
Liu prioritized building a 700-person infrastructure team before scaling models, recognizing that compute/data systems are the bottleneck, not model architecture.

**Quote:** "我去了之後我們當時做了幾個事情...一個就是極致的解放，然後做Infra，做大規模的Infra，Kruz應該是當時做的做增加式企業里面Infra團隊算是最大規模的一個...有700人的Infra團隊" [00:27:28] (Translation: "When I joined we did several things... one was extreme emancipation, building infra, large-scale infra. Cruise should have had the largest infra team among autonomous driving companies at that time... a 700-person infra team")

### Insight 4: CEO Resource Allocation Signals Must Match Stated Strategy
He Xiaopeng's willingness to immediately approve massive GPU spending without detailed business cases signaled genuine commitment to AI transformation, enabling decisive action.

**Quote:** "當你想做一件事情的時候...我們去年的時候跟男人說我要做這個事，這個事就是要去做大模型...當時沒有任何彈幕，然後也沒有任何的結果，然後你打算說這麼多GPU這麼多錢你怎麼辦，打算說好，就完了" [00:35:04] (Translation: "When you want to do something... last year I told the boss I want to do this, build large models... at that time there were no demos, no results, I said I need this many GPUs this much money what do you think, he said okay, done")

### Insight 5: Problem-First Research Over Paper-First Research
Liu advocates starting from real production problems rather than academic papers, only consulting literature after identifying concrete issues requiring solutions.

**Quote:** "我不太喜歡別人過化一個框架就告訴你，這是你的指導你去用話的，而更多的是，這就是真實你要解決的問題，你怎麼能從這個問題中找出不一樣的東西來把它變成了你要去做的微色" [01:04:00] (Translation: "I don't like when someone gives you a framework and says this is your guidance - instead, this is the real problem you need to solve, how can you find something different from this problem and turn it into research you need to do")

## 6. Overlooked Insights

### Insight 1: OEM Data Advantage Creates Winner-Take-Most Dynamics
Liu's emphasis on data collection speed (overnight problem identification to next-day data collection) suggests autonomous driving may have stronger network effects than commonly understood. If OEMs can iterate models based on yesterday's failures by tomorrow, third-party suppliers face an insurmountable disadvantage regardless of technical prowess. This implies the industry structure will consolidate around OEMs rather than fragment into specialized AI suppliers.

**Quote:** "比如說我今天有一個模型上出現了一個問題，我可以自動化一個去找到我數據分布的這個新格爾的point，就哪些地方是我數據比較缺失的地方，迅速地讓我的車隊明天就會收上來數據，還是利用起來，新的這樣一個避換，這個事情的主機場只有主機場能做到" [01:13:38] (Translation: "For example, if my model has a problem today, I can automatically find the singular point in my data distribution, identify where data is lacking, quickly get my fleet to collect data tomorrow and utilize it, this new iteration - only OEMs can do this")

### Insight 2: The AI Talent Competition Is Shifting From Credentials to Risk Tolerance
Liu mentioned recruiting young people for "Fresh New Brain" rather than experienced researchers. Combined with his extreme work hours (2-3 AM bedtime) and organizational flattening, this suggests the competitive bottleneck isn't finding AI PhDs but finding people willing to operate at unsustainable intensity with high career risk. The winners may be companies that can attract/retain people willing to "睡覺時間變得更少" (sacrifice sleep time) rather than those offering the best research environments.

**Quote:** "我們是希望去有很多年輕的人加入我們，他們會提供很多新鮮的想法，會有更充沛的經歷...我們叫Fresh New Brain...他往往可以跳脫出我們現在也有的況下就想問題" [01:19:32] (Translation: "We hope many young people join us, they provide fresh ideas, have more abundant energy... we call it Fresh New Brain... they can often think outside our current situation")
