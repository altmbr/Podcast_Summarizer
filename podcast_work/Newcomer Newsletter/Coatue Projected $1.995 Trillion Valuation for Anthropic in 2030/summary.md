# Newsletter Summary: Coatue's Anthropic Valuation Projections

> **Note:** This article is largely paywalled. The summary below is based exclusively on the publicly available portion of the article.

---

## 1. Key Themes

### Theme 1: Anthropic Is Being Valued on a Multi-Trillion Dollar Trajectory
Coatue is underwriting a path to near-$2 trillion in value for Anthropic within a five-year window, representing one of the most aggressive valuation frameworks ever applied to a private company.

> "By 2031, Coatue projected that Anthropic would be generating $48 billion in EBITDA profits on $200 billion in revenue, with $224 billion of ARR. That would give Anthropic a 41x forward EBITDA multiple at a projected $1.995 trillion valuation in 2030."

### Theme 2: Massive Near-Term Losses Are Being Priced In as Acceptable
Investors are explicitly modeling deep losses as a feature, not a bug — a sign that the AI infrastructure buildout phase is being treated similarly to early cloud or e-commerce investing.

> "Coatue estimated that Anthropic would lose $14 billion in EBITDA on $18 billion in revenue in 2026, ending the year with $30 billion in annualized run-rate revenue."

### Theme 3: Anthropic May Already Be Outpacing Optimistic Projections
Early revenue performance suggests that even the bull-case models used to justify a $380B valuation could be conservative, adding further urgency to the investment thesis.

> "Most recently, it's been reported that Anthropic is at $19 billion in ARR, which could suggest Anthropic is already running ahead of Coatue's expectations."

### Theme 4: Large Private Rounds Are Replacing Public Markets as Price Discovery Mechanisms
The scale of late-stage private financing means investors must rely on fund-internal modeling — not public market comps — to assess value, creating both opacity and opportunity.

> "This is a moment in time when some of America's most valuable, growing technology companies remain private... Coatue offered a rare look at how it justifies Anthropic's heady valuations."

---

## 2. Contrarian Perspectives

### The "Losses Don't Matter" Framing May Be Strategically Justified
Conventional investing penalizes $14B EBITDA losses. Here, Coatue is framing them as a transitional cost on the path to $48B in EBITDA — a bet that the unit economics will eventually mirror or exceed those of the largest software businesses ever built.

> "Coatue estimated that Anthropic would lose $14 billion in EBITDA on $18 billion in revenue in 2026... By 2031, Coatue projected that Anthropic would be generating $48 billion in EBITDA profits on $200 billion in revenue."

The implied swing from -$14B to +$48B EBITDA in roughly five years is an extraordinary assumption — but the early ARR trajectory appears to be validating the revenue side of the model.

### A 41x Forward EBITDA Multiple Signals AI Is Being Priced Like a Category-Defining Platform
A 41x forward EBITDA multiple is typically reserved for the highest-growth, highest-margin software businesses. Applying it to a company still deeply unprofitable suggests Coatue believes Anthropic will achieve near-monopoly or oligopoly economics.

> "That would give Anthropic a 41x forward EBITDA multiple at a projected $1.995 trillion valuation in 2030."

## 6. Overlooked Insights

### The Valuation Gap Between Entry and Exit Is Itself an Investment Signal
Coatue invested at a $380B valuation while projecting a $2T outcome — roughly a 5-6x return on valuation alone, before accounting for dilution. This spread suggests that even at seemingly stretched entry prices, top funds see substantial upside in frontier AI.

> "In February, Coatue co-led a $30 billion Series G investment in Anthropic that valued it at $380 billion... Coatue told the investors it was pitching that Anthropic could be worth $2.413 trillion in 2031."

### Coatue Is Using Investor Pitch Decks as a Capital-Raising Tool, Not Just Diligence
The fact that these projections were presented to *prospective investors* — not just used internally — means the $2T figure is partly a fundraising narrative, and should be read with that incentive structure in mind.

> "In a presentation to prospective investors in January, Coatue offered a rare look at how it justifies Anthropic's heady valuations."
