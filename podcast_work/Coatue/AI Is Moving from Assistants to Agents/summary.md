## 1. Key Themes

### The Shift from AI Assistance to AI Agency
The central thesis is a generational change in how AI operates — moving from reactive, suggestion-based tools to autonomous, end-to-end executors.

> *"We're witnessing a massive shift in AI, from merely assisting with work to doing the work itself. While the previous generation of technology focused on assistance through autocomplete, suggestions, and drafts, the new paradigm centers on agentic systems that execute tasks end-to-end."*

### Coding as the Proof-of-Concept Vertical for Agentic AI
Coatue identifies software development as the first measurable, undeniable example of the agentic transition — and frames it as a template for broader industry adoption.

> *"Coding is the first vertical where this pattern is undeniably clear, and it serves as a blueprint for what we expect to see across industries."*

### 2026 as the Inflection Point for the Agentic Era
The article marks early 2026 as a distinct acceleration moment, attributing it to new model capabilities that crossed a threshold enabling true agency.

> *"The data shows a sharp acceleration in early 2026, as new models unlocked the 'agentic' era."*

## 2. Contrarian Perspectives

### Adoption Metrics Suggest the Agentic Shift Is Already Happening — Not Coming
The consensus framing around AI agents is often forward-looking ("agents are the future"), but Coatue's data implies the transition is already measurably underway, not theoretical.

> *"Claude Code downloads soared 70x in under a year, while Codex installations rose 7x in seven months."*

These are present-tense, quantified adoption figures — not projections — suggesting investors and operators who are still in "wait and see" mode may already be behind the curve.
